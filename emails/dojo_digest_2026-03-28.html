April Classes — IM Tatev Abrahamyan and GM Josh Friedel are returning to continue their series on endgames and middlegames. IM Kostya Kavutskiy will continue his 1.d4 starter repertoire and calculation classes. GM Jesse Kraai will continue Chernev's book Logical Chess Move by Move.
 
 

Join all classes and learn more here!
 

Square Color Drill — Do you struggle with visualization? We have a new drill available on our website to practice visualizing the board. You'll be prompted with the name of a square and need to quickly answer which color it is. This is just the first step in a series of visualization drills we are adding to the site, and is a direct result of Jesse's visualization class. 
 

Game & Profile Review — Interested in having your games and training habits analyzed by one of our senseis? Join the Game & Profile Review! You'll get placed with a cohort of similarly-rated players. Each week, you meet with your peers and analyze one member's game. A few days later, you meet with the sensei, who reviews the same game and answers the questions from your peer review session.

Being a Chess Professional

GM Felix Blohberger joins Dojo Talks to discuss the realities of being a modern chess professional, including balancing playing, coaching, content creation, and course development while pursuing a long-term goal of reaching 2600.

How to Improve Your Board Visualization

GM Jesse Kraai explains a few drills you can use to improve your board visualization and highlights a student's recent improvements.

Are Group Classes Better?

Are group classes actually better than studying alone? Jesse explores how commitment, peer motivation, and shared training environments can dramatically increase consistency and improvement in chess study.

Should Draw Offers Be Banned?

A lively Dojo Talks debate on whether draw offers should be banned in chess. The episode covers the history of draw offers, the role of tournament culture and incentives, the Dubov–Nepo controversy, and whether changing draw rules would actually improve fighting chess.

Chessboxing and the Dreams of James Canty

FM James Canty III joins Dojo Talks to talk about becoming the World Chessboxing Champion and his push for IM and GM norms.

Does Having Kids Ruin Your Chess?

In this episode of Dojo Talks, we explore how having children impacts chess performance, from real examples of world champions to personal experiences as chess dads. With Hikaru Nakamura entering the Candidates as a new father, the question is more relevant than ever.

Lift Heavy, Win More Chess Games

GM Kraai breaks down the surprising connection between strength training and chess performance—from managing stress and heart rate during games to improving focus, discipline, and decision-making at the board. He also compares cardio vs. weightlifting and explains why structured training might be the real key to improvement.
